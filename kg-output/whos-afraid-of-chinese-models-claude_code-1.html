Analysis

The Return of Marginal Costs

Kellogg, Aggregation Theory, and the return of marginal costs

Thompson recalls that Aggregation Theory rests on the Internet's zero marginal costs, but argues AI is reviving old universal principles: the X debate over Kimi K3 shows marginal costs and industry structure are back in a big way.

COGS versus R&D, tokens versus intelligence

R&D is a fixed cost independent of revenue, but COGS — the cost of running inference — scales directly with it. Tokens are not fungible across models; what's fungible is the correct answer a model's tokens ultimately produce, which is why raw per-token pricing (like Kimi K3's $3/$15 versus Sol's $5/$30 per million tokens) can be a misleading comparison.

Understanding commodity markets

In a commodity market, the market-clearing price tracks the marginal cost of the highest-cost supplier needed to meet demand — rewarding low-cost producers and threatening high-cost ones with bankruptcy. Right now intelligence isn't priced this way because compute, not demand, is the binding constraint.

Frontier lab paranoia

Frontier labs remain anchored in a training-cost mindset, are racing to compound data advantages, are integrating up the stack (Claude Code, Codex), and — in Anthropic's case — carry an ideological commitment to being the only trustworthy steward of powerful AI.

China's motivation and the distillation question

Xi Jinping's speech ties AI openness to AI's move into the physical world, where China's robotics lead compounds. Because U.S. open-weight makers must honor frontier labs' anti-distillation terms of service, they end up distilling via a Chinese detour — a recurring structural disadvantage Thompson thinks U.S. copyright policy should fix.

The One Real Risk

The Hugging Face Breach and the Case to Be Afraid

Hugging Face's production infrastructure was breached by an autonomous AI agent. U.S. frontier-model guardrails initially blocked incident responders — the guardrails "cannot distinguish an incident responder from an attacker" — so defenders instead ran China's open-source GLM 5.2 on their own infrastructure to analyze 17,000+ attacker log entries. Thompson's conclusion: U.S. defenders need equal access to Fable and Sol for cybersecurity work, rather than a China-dependent fallback.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is COGS, and why does it matter for open weight AI models?

COGS (cost of goods sold) is the cost of running inference on a model, and unlike R&D — a fixed cost independent of revenue — COGS scales directly with revenue: 1,000x revenue means roughly 1,000x COGS.

Why are "free" open weight models like Kimi K3 not actually free to serve?

Downloading open weights avoids R&D spend, but running inference still costs money; Kimi K3 charges $3 per million input tokens and $15 per million output tokens, cheaper than Sol but not free.

What does Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang mean by "token factories"?

Huang frames Nvidia GPUs as model-agnostic token generators, making metrics like tokens-per-second and token cost the basis for decision-making — a framing confounded by reasoning-era chain-of-thought token explosion.

Why are tokens not a commodity across different AI models?

A token from one model is not fungible with a token from another; what is fungible is the intelligence — the correct answer — that different models require different numbers of tokens to reach.

What factors determine the COGS of intelligence?

Model footprint, inference efficiency (e.g. Mixture-of-Experts), memory efficiency (KV cache requirements), serving efficiency (batching and scheduling), and token efficiency.

How do commodity markets determine pricing, per Thompson's supplier example?

In a commodity market everyone charges the market-clearing price, set by the marginal cost of the highest-cost supplier needed to satisfy demand; the highest-cost supplier risks bankruptcy from its fixed costs.

Why does Thompson think the reaction to Kimi K3 is overblown?

Demand for frontier intelligence still exceeds compute supply, so Anthropic and OpenAI's high prices reflect a compute-driven price umbrella rather than Chinese models being cheaper on a true marginal-cost basis.

Why do frontier labs seem panicked about Chinese models?

Labs are anchored in a training-cost-dominated mindset, applied intelligence compounds via collected data, labs are integrating up the stack (Claude Code, Codex), and Anthropic's safety ideology adds an extra edge.

What is China's strategic motivation for open-weight AI models?

Xi Jinping's speech ties AI openness to AI moving from the digital into the physical world, where China holds a robotics lead; commoditizing complements weakens U.S. frontier labs while strengthening China's ecosystem.

What is the distillation question, and why does it advantage Chinese labs?

Chinese labs use frontier-lab models as reinforcement-learning teachers; because U.S. open-weight makers must obey anti-distillation terms of service, they end up distilling via a Chinese detour, a recurring structural disadvantage.

What cybersecurity incident shows the real risk of restricting frontier model access?

Hugging Face's incident responders were blocked by U.S. frontier-model guardrails that could not distinguish a responder from an attacker, so they ran China's open-source GLM 5.2 to analyze 17,000+ attack logs instead.

What policy changes does Thompson recommend for U.S. AI competitiveness?

Pass a law affirming training-data collection is fair use and barring anti-distillation terms of service for U.S. companies; loosen restrictions on defenders' cybersecurity use of Fable and Sol.

Glossary

Key Terms

Aggregation Theory
Thompson's framework holding that zero marginal costs on the Internet produce centralization and scale where controlling demand matters more than distributing supply.
COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)
The variable cost of running inference on an AI model, which scales directly with revenue, unlike fixed R&D spend.
R&D as a Fixed Expense
Research and development spending on training a model, independent of the revenue that model later generates.
Token Factory
Jensen Huang's framing of Nvidia GPUs as model-agnostic infrastructure that generates tokens as fast and efficiently as possible.
Mixture-of-Experts (MoE)
An architectural choice that reduces the computation required per generated token, improving inference efficiency.
KV Cache
Memory used during inference to store key/value attention state; reducing KV cache needs allows more concurrent requests and better GPU utilization.
Commodity Market
A market where everyone sells the same fungible good at a single price set by the marginal cost of the highest-cost supplier needed to meet demand.
Open Weight Model
A model whose trained weights are downloadable and self-hostable, in contrast to a closed frontier model served only via API.
Distillation
Using a stronger frontier model's outputs as a reinforcement-learning teacher to rapidly and cheaply improve a smaller or newer model.
Frontier Lab
An AI company, such as Anthropic or OpenAI, operating at the state-of-the-art capability level and serving models exclusively via closed API.
