Analysis

The Great Flattening

The machine can't cite what it can't tell apart

Companies relying on AI-generated averaged content become invisible to AI search engines because such content lacks the distinguishing characteristics generative models need in order to cite it.

The loop tightening inside content teams right now

Teams generating high-volume AI content produce fluent but undifferentiated material that engines never cite, prompting them to increase volume further and tighten an ineffective cycle.

The companies the engines keep choosing

AI-driven referral traffic and conversion rates are concentrating around a small set of companies whose content is distinctive enough to be cited by generative engines.

Run the retrieval test

Readers are urged to test their AI search visibility directly by asking ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini the questions their target buyers ask, then counting appearances.

The 95-5 rule just acquired a machine

Ehrenberg-Bass's 95-5 rule, describing the small share of buyers actively in-market at any time, now interacts with generative engines that decide which brands those in-market buyers ever see.

Guide

How to Test and Improve Your AI Search Visibility

  1. Identify five buyer questions

    Identify five questions your target buyers ask before building a shortlist of vendors or solutions.

  2. Run the retrieval test

    Ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini each of the five questions and record whether your brand appears in the answers.

  3. Audit recent content for distinctiveness

    Review your recently published content and identify sentences that contain original data, unique claims, or distinctive positions.

  4. Add concrete statistics

    Add concrete statistics to underperforming content; this alone was found to lift AI answer visibility by roughly 40%.

  5. Add sourced quotations and attribution

    Add quotations and attribute claims to named sources; quotations lifted visibility nearly 30%, and attribution produced gains exceeding 100% for previously non-ranked pages.

  6. Stop optimizing for keywords

    Deprioritize keyword optimization, since it was found to make AI answer visibility worse, and prioritize distinguishability instead.

  7. Track brand-mention frequency

    Track brand-mention frequency, not just backlinks, as the primary key performance indicator for AI search visibility.

Glossary

Key Terms

Great Flattening
The homogenization of AI-generated content into indistinguishable, fluent-but-uncitable material that generative engines decline to reference.
AI Overviews
Google's AI-generated summary results shown above traditional organic search listings.
Answer Engine Optimization
The practice of optimizing content for citation by generative AI answer engines rather than ranking in traditional search results.
Citation Rate
The frequency with which a generative AI engine references a given source when constructing its answers.
95-5 Rule
An Ehrenberg-Bass finding that roughly 95% of category buyers are not in-market at a given time, with only about 5% actively purchasing.
Generative Engine
An AI system, such as ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity, that synthesizes answers from retrieved sources rather than returning a list of links.
Brand Mention
An unlinked textual reference to a brand name that generative AI models use as a visibility signal, found to predict AI visibility three times more strongly than backlinks.
Content Loop
A self-reinforcing cycle in which teams produce high-volume, undifferentiated AI content that engines never cite, prompting further volume increases.
Retrieval Test
A methodology of running large batches of queries through generative engines to measure which sources get cited in the results.
AI Referral Traffic
Website visits arriving from links or citations surfaced within AI chat or generative search-engine answers.
